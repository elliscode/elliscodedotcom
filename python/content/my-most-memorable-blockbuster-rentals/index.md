If you grew up in the previous millennium, some of your most cherished memories probably involved a video rental store, such as Blockbuster. Some of my favorite memories are going to Blockbuster with your best friends on a Friday night to pick out a movie, a game, and a ton of candy for a sleepover. One of my friends and I had a tradition of picking out the most terrifying horror movies we could find, and seeing if we could finish them, which fostered my lifelong love for the genre.

However not every trip was a winner, and not every rental was worth the few dollars you paid for it, especially when it came to games. Whether you went looking for the newest game, and walked out with a consolation rental cause you didn't want to leave empty handed, or got drawn in by the awesome box art, only to discover a pungent turd of a game when you got home, disappointment was part and parcel of the rental experience.

For some reason, literal decades later, I have extremely vivid memories of renting a select few titles from Blockbuster over and over again, and I'm honestly not sure why. I tried replaying these games as an adult, and was kind of blown away by how bad they were. But something drew me to them, and hopefully we can figure out why together.
